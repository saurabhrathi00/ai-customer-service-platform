1. Product Overview

1.1 Product Vision
An AI-powered 24/7 customer service platform that acts as an intelligent middleman between small businesses and their customers. When a customer calls a business number, the AI intercepts the call, answers queries using business-specific knowledge, and ensures no customer goes unattended — even outside business hours.

1.2 Platform Type
B2C platform — the company acts as the intermediary connecting businesses (clients) with their end customers.

1.3 Problem Statement
	•	Small businesses cannot afford 24/7 customer support staff.
	•	Customers calling after hours get no response, leading to lost business.
	•	Businesses miss follow-up opportunities due to lack of call tracking.
	•	Setting up AI voice systems directly (e.g., Twilio) requires technical expertise small businesses don't have.

1.4 Solution
	•	Provide a plug-and-play AI voice assistant for each business.
	•	Each business gets a dedicated Twilio phone number.
	•	AI answers customer calls 24/7 using only that business's knowledge base.
	•	Complete isolation — Business A's AI never accesses Business B's data.
	•	Call summaries, customer details, and interest ratings saved automatically for business follow-up.

3. Functional Requirements

3.1 Business Onboarding
3.1.1 Registration & Authentication
	•	Business can self-register on the platform (email + password).
	•	Email verification required before onboarding.
	•	Secure login/logout functionality.
	•	Password reset via email.

3.1.2 Business Profile Setup
	•	After registration, business fills in their profile:
	•	Business name
	•	Business type / category
	•	Business description
	•	Location / address
	•	Operating hours
	•	Contact email
	•	Each business is assigned a unique Business ID (primary key) upon registration.

3.1.3 Phone Number Assignment
	•	A unique Twilio phone number is assigned to each business upon onboarding.
	•	A business can have multiple phone numbers associated with it.
	•	Businesses do NOT need to change their existing number — the Twilio number is an additional number for AI interaction.
	•	Phone number is stored and mapped to the Business ID in the database.

3.1.4 Knowledge Base Upload
	•	Business can upload knowledge materials after onboarding.
	•	Supported file types: PDF, TXT, Images (JPG, PNG).
	•	Files stored in cloud blob storage (AWS S3 or equivalent).
	•	Expected size: a few KBs to max 4-5 MB per business.
	•	Business can update or delete knowledge materials at any time.
	•	Each file is tagged to its Business ID — no cross-business access possible.

3.2 Customer Call Flow
3.2.1 Call Routing & Identification
	•	Customer dials the Twilio number assigned to a business.
	•	Twilio webhook fires to our system with the incoming number.
	•	System looks up which business owns that Twilio number.
	•	Loads ONLY the knowledge base of that specific business.
	•	Strict isolation — no data from other businesses is ever loaded.

3.2.2 AI Conversation
	•	AI greets the customer on behalf of the business.
	•	AI answers customer questions using only the loaded business knowledge.
	•	AI collects basic customer details during conversation:
	•	Customer name
	•	Customer phone number (auto-captured via Twilio)
	•	Nature of query

3.2.3 Scenario Handling

Scenario
AI Behaviour
AI has sufficient answer
Continues conversation, answers queries, ends call normally.
AI cannot answer / lacks info
Responds: "Our team will get back to you within 24 hours." Saves callback request.
Customer explicitly asks for human
Same as above — saves callback request with high priority.

3.2.4 Post-Call Feedback
	•	At the end of every call, AI asks for quick feedback.
	•	Customer is prompted: "Were your questions answered? Press 1 for Yes, Press 2 for No."
	•	Feedback response is recorded and saved against the call record.

3.2.5 Post-Call Data Saved
	•	Call summary (AI-generated).
	•	Customer basic details (name, phone, query type).
	•	Feedback score.
	•	Interest rating score (calculated based on business configuration).
	•	Timestamp of call.
	•	Call duration.
	•	Whether callback was requested.

3.4 Business Dashboard
3.4.1 Recent Calls Section (Last 24 Hours)
	•	Displays all calls received in the last 24 hours.
	•	Columns shown:
	•	Customer Name
	•	Phone Number
	•	Query Summary (AI-generated)
	•	Call Duration
	•	Time of Call
	•	Interest Rating (star display)
	•	Feedback Given

3.4.2 Priority Callback List
	•	Shows customers who:
	•	AI could NOT answer their query, OR
	•	Explicitly requested to speak to a human.
	•	Sorted by time in ASCENDING order (earliest first).
	•	Columns shown:
	•	Time of Call
	•	Customer Name
	•	Phone Number
	•	Their Query / Reason for Callback

3.4.3 Knowledge Base Management
	•	Business can view all uploaded files.
	•	Can upload new files (PDF, TXT, Images).
	•	Can delete existing files.
	•	Updated knowledge is reflected in AI responses for future calls.

3.4.4 Rating Configuration
	•	Business can adjust interest rating scoring rules.
	•	Each signal weight can be updated independently.
	•	Changes apply to future calls only.

4. Non-Functional Requirements

Requirement

Strict multi-tenant isolation — Business A can never access Business B's data.

Architecture must support multiple businesses independently.

Knowledge base files stored in S3 / cloud blob storage per business.

Business login secured with hashed passwords. API endpoints authenticated.
